Coursework Information

SYDE 542 is a course in interface design. Coursework to date has covered user requirements review, work domain analysis, navigation, wirteframing, icons and colour usage. As we work through design examples, selected exercises will be posted here to showcase the development of UI design principles as I progress through the course.

Most design exercises will focus on the CARE-RATE project, the subject of my thesis work here at the University of Waterloo. CARE-RATE is an online assistive technology being developed as part of the AGE-WELL program, Canada's technology and aging network. CARE-RATE focuses on dementia caregivers, aiming to provide a resource that caregivers can talk to and draw support from even when they don't know what they're not aware of what they're searching for. Unlike Google or traditional search engines where users know how to frame a query and know what they're looking for, many caregivers are non-native computer users and may be unaware that products or information exist to help them. CARE-RATE aims to act as an undirected search engine, able to talk with caregivers as they describe problems or issues and find helpful resources to support these caregivers. Creating a UI that accomodates conversation and undirected search is the crux of my thesis work here.
